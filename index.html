How to read this

Two trend horizons

Following UK Biodiversity Indicator practice, every species gets a short-term read (latest complete year vs the preceding 5-year average) and, once enough history exists, a long-term read (earliest 3 years on record vs the most recent 3). They can disagree — a species can be down this year but improving over the long run, or vice versa.

Observation counts

Sourced from GBIF, filtered to a Northern Ireland bounding box plus a UK country filter. These reflect recorded observations, not population size — a fall can mean fewer organisms, fewer people recording, or both.

GenBank sequences

Sourced from NCBI GenBank. Tracks research and sequencing attention, not field abundance — counts almost never fall, so they're described as steady, accelerating, or stalled.

Pest & pathogen status

Regulatory labels here are a starting reference, not a live legal register. Always verify against the NI Plant Health Risk Register and the Bee Diseases and Pests Control Order (NI) 2007.

Data refresh & provenance

An automated, publicly-viewable job re-fetches every figure on a schedule and commits the result — the full pipeline, thresholds and species list are all on GitHub for anyone to audit.
