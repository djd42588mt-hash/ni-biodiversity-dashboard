How to read this

Comprehensive vs curated figures

"Trends by group" and the map use every GBIF-recorded species in Northern Ireland for that group (queried by taxonomic class/kingdom/phylum) — a genuinely complete picture, not limited to this dashboard's tracked list. The species browser, compare tool, alerts and pest watch focus on a curated ~30-species watchlist instead, since those need individual species-level detail. Both are shown so neither is mistaken for the other.

Two trend horizons

Following UK Biodiversity Indicator practice, every species and group gets a short-term read (latest complete year vs the preceding 5-year average) and a long-term read (earliest years on record vs the most recent). They can disagree — down this year but improving over the long run, or vice versa.

Observation counts

Sourced from GBIF, filtered to a Northern Ireland bounding box plus a UK country filter. These reflect recorded observations, not population size — a fall can mean fewer organisms, fewer people recording, or both.

The map

Grid cells are built from a fixed-size sample of recent records per group (not the full dataset), so they show relative concentration, not a corrected density estimate — recording effort itself is uneven across Northern Ireland (more visits to accessible/populated areas), and this map does not adjust for that.

GenBank sequences

Sourced from NCBI GenBank. Tracks research and sequencing attention, not field abundance — counts almost never fall, so they're described as steady, accelerating, or stalled, and shown even when flat so effort is visible either way.

Pest & pathogen status

These entries are hand-curated by cross-referencing published DAERA/DEFRA policy documents (each card shows when it was last reviewed) — this dashboard does not currently ingest the Risk Register's own data file automatically. Always verify against the live NI Plant Health Risk Register and the Bee Diseases and Pests Control Order (NI) 2007 before treating anything here as current.

Data refresh & provenance

An automated, publicly-viewable job re-fetches every figure on a schedule and commits the result — the full pipeline, thresholds and species list are all on GitHub for anyone to audit.
